Why Recycling Matters

Conserves Resources

Recycling reduces the need for raw materials, saving trees, water, and energy. Every ton of paper recycled saves 17 trees and 7,000 gallons of water.

Reduces Pollution

Recycling decreases air and water pollution by reducing the need for new material production. It also prevents waste from ending up in landfills and oceans.

Fights Climate Change

Recycling reduces greenhouse gas emissions by decreasing energy consumption in manufacturing processes and preventing methane release from landfills.

Understanding Waste Segregation

Wet Waste

Organic waste like food scraps, garden waste, and biodegradable materials that can be composted to create nutrient-rich soil for sustainable agriculture.

Dry Waste

Non-biodegradable materials like plastics, paper, glass, and metals that can be recycled and processed into new products through proper waste management.

Hazardous Waste

Dangerous materials like batteries, electronics, and chemicals that require special handling and disposal to prevent environmental contamination.
